Material Design Engineering and UX Prototyping

I joined the Material Design team in 2015. My work on the team has ranged from writing production code for user interface components to prototyping.

When prototyping I've worked closely with designers to optimize motion and visual design. Below you'll find videos of work I've done on the team.

iOS Material Design Components

Open-sourced user interface components used throughout Google iOS apps

Material Design Components are production quality user interface components that are open-sourced on Github. These components are used in most Google iOS apps. As a member of the MDC engineering team, I played a major role in building and maintaining a number of components (shown in the videos below).

  • Activity Indicator

    Visual indications of an app loading content using Material Design animation and layout.

  • Animation Timing

    Material Design animation timing curves.

  • Bottom App Bar

    A specialized bottom navigation bar that includes a floating action button for a primary action.

  • Bottom Navigation

    Bottom navigation with custom parameterization that adheres to Material Design standards.

  • Ink

    The Ink component provides a radial action in the form of a visual ripple of ink expanding outward from the user's touch.

  • Shadows

    Implementations of the Material Design specifications for elevation and shadows.

  • Snackbars

    Snackbars provide brief feedback about an operation through a message at the bottom of the screen.

  • Themes

    A theming framework that enables all user interface components to be colorized easily according to Material Design standards.

  • Token Field

    Token fields allow tokenizable text entry so text can be changed into manipulatable chip (token) elements.

Material Design Product Demos

Showcasing how MDC enables great design

I built a number of demo apps to illustrate how MDC allows design and engineering to collaborate together and quickly build apps with great design adhering to Material Design standards.

I worked closely with both designers and engineers to ensure the demo apps met Material Design's high standards for great design and high quality code. These apps are the primary example apps used by developers when learning how to use MDC.

M2 Feasibility Prototypes

Prototyping design explorations and assessing platform limitations

M2 is the successor to Material Design. I created a number of design feasibility prototypes to explore if proposed M2 design concepts could be implemented on the iOS platform. This exercise served to inform engineering on how to proceed with implementation and if designs needed to be adjusted.

I worked closely with designers to explore how to implement motion and visual design on iOS using Apple's graphics and animation APIs — working within the limitations of the iOS platform.

  • Curves

    Explorations of custom animation three point timing curves.

  • Filters

    Explorations of how to apply Photoshop-like filters to images in real-time.

  • Ink

    Explorations of adding ink to components on various dynamic surfaces.

  • Ink Calendar

    Explorations of how ink trails can be added to a calendar component.

  • Liquid Header

    Explorations of adding liquid and spring effects to the app bar header.

  • Spring Header

    Explorations of adding spring-like physics to an app bar header.

  • Shapes

    Explorations of using curved and cut shapes within a user interface.

  • Tonal Shadows

    Explorations of how color shades affect shadows in the user interface.

  • Scrubber

    Explorations of a proposed update to video scrubbers.
