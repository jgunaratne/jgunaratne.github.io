Case Study: Google Bard I/O Preview

  • Role Product Designer and Prototyper
  • Stakeholders Product and UX Directors, CEO
  • Timeframe 6 months

Problem Statement

Google is losing market share to competitors in the LLM (local, live, and mobile) space. The company needs to quickly iterate on a new product to address this gap.

Research

I conducted a competitive analysis of Google's competitors in the LLM space. I also conducted a heuristic evaluation of Google's existing products in the LLM space.

Personas

I created personas based on the research I conducted. I also created a user journey map for each persona.

Ideation

I created a series of sketches and wireframes to explore different design directions. I also created a series of paper prototypes to test with users.

Mocks and Prototypes

I created a series of high-fidelity mocks and prototypes to test with users. I also created a series of high-fidelity mocks and prototypes to present to stakeholders.

User Testing

I conducted a series of user tests to validate the design direction. I also conducted a series of user tests to validate the design direction.

Final Design

I created a series of high-fidelity mocks and prototypes to test with users. I also created a series of high-fidelity mocks and prototypes to present to stakeholders.

Results

  • Improved speed of design iteration — Use of agile prototyping in design helped address internal and external criticism that Google was not moving fast enough in the LLM space.
  • Influenced senior leadership — Prototype presented to CEO helped shape and influence leadership decision-making throughout Search and Ads.
  • Received recognition from senior leadership — Product and design leadership praised the high quality deliverables that addressed rapidly changing market conditions.
  • Created positive media attention — Google I/O presentation influenced media portrayal of Google as quickly closing gaps with competition.
