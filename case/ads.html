Case Study: Google Ads + ML

  • Role Product Design Lead
  • Stakeholders Product SVP
  • Timeframe 9 months

Problem

The Google Ads team had a twofold goal: first, to figure out how to harness the power of machine learning technology in order to help client firms identify suitable ad categories for their ad listings; second, to explore ways that client firms could enhance the design of their ad content and visuals based on these ad categories.

Despite having a skilled machine learning team, the challenge lay in the fact that the tools for obtaining machine learning recommendations required understanding technical jargon and programming expertise. Recognizing the need for a more accessible approach, my task was to develop a user-friendly interface so that non-technical users from ad client firms could effectively utilize these advanced machine learning tools.

The initial machine learning tools for ads required users to understand technical jargon and have programming expertise.

Research

In the early stages working with our researcher, we found that ad clients often struggled with choosing the right ad categories and frequently missed out on selecting suitable secondary ad categories, limiting the potential for maximizing their ad monetization. When we presented the initial prototype to users, further research revealed that they desired an additional method to engage with the machine learning data. Users expressed a need for ad imagery recommendations, recognizing the potential benefits of such insights in enhancing their advertising strategies. This feedback emphasized the importance of not only refining category selection, but also integrating helpful recommendations for ad visuals to improve overall ad performance.

Personas

Working with research we defined our target user as a non-technical user working at a mid-sized ad agency who relies on Google Ads enterprise tools to craft and publish ads through the Google Ad systems. Our objective was to allow this user to use the power of machine learning technology to streamline their ad categorizations and visuals.

The tool targeted non-technical users and provided an introduction screen with a brief overview of the tool's machine learning capabilities.

Ideation

In the initial phase, the product manager, machine learning engineer and I focused on developing a system that could analyze ad imagery uploaded by ad clients and generate machine learning-based recommendations for ad categories. As we progressed to the second iteration, user feedback from clients in our research highlighted new user needs. Ad firm clients expressed a desire for the ability to leverage the machine learning ad category system not just for categorization but also for recommending imagery for their ads. I worked closely with an ML engineer to understand the technical limitations of machine learning and the quality we could expect from ML in image recommendations for ads.

The initial mocks and prototype focused on ad category recommendations.

Mocks and Prototypes

In the early stages of development, we used initial static mock-ups to illustrate the theoretical flow of ad recommendations. Recognizing the significant impact of machine learning (ML) recommendations on user perception during testing, it became crucial to incorporate these recommendations into prototypes. I crafted two prototypes to explore this further. The first prototype focused on displaying ad category recommendations, reflecting what a client uploaded into the system and returned real ML recommendations generated by our ML systems. The second prototype took a step further by showing imagery recommendations based on the ad categories selected by the client user. These recommendations were generated through machine learning algorithms, adding a dynamic and data-driven layer to enhance the overall user experience. These prototypes allowed us to gauge the practicality and user reception of ML-driven recommendations in shaping the advertising product.

Inappropriate ad uploads required designing an elegant way to censor images.

User Testing

With qualitative feedback from a dozen users at ad firms, the initial prototype research revealed that users found the ad category recommendations to be beneficial. However, it also highlighted a more crucial requirement: ad clients needed a tool that could assist them in the creative process and facilitate the discovery of imagery tailored to specific ad categories. This insight highlighted the importance of expanding our approach beyond categorization alone, emphasizing the need to integrate features that could enhance the creative aspect of ad creation. My focus shifted towards developing a solution that not only provided useful recommendations for ad categories but also in aiding the creative process of ad clients in sourcing relevant and impactful imagery for their advertisements.

Ad categories were generated through machine learning algorithms, but not sufficient for the creative process and users asked for help with imagery selection as well.

The second prototype focused on imagery recommendations based on the ad categories selected by the client user.

Final Design

The final design solution not only offered ad category recommendations but also introduced a user-friendly feature enabling users to input an ad category and receive suggestions from a stock photo library. This addition aimed to facilitate the creative process by providing a convenient resource for finding relevant and engaging imagery. The transformation was significant; we converted a command-line product, initially accessible only to highly technical machine learning engineers, into a tool accessible to non-technical users at creative ad firms. The impact of this evolution was profound, garnering praise from a Senior VP of product in ads, who hailed the released product as the most innovative in recent years.

Results

Accomplishments

  • Strengthened partnership with engineering — Helped the engineering team gain a better understanding of how to apply machine learning to a practical problem.
  • Challenged and overcame organizational conventions — By using agile prototyping and addressing data and privacy concerns, the team overcame organizational inertia and launched a new product in record time.
  • Received recognition from senior leadership — An Ads Senior VP lauded the product as "the most innovative Ads product in recent years."
  • Influenced leadership by spearheading external user research — Research obtained from the prototype helped the team understand the value of ML ad category suggestions and influenced leadership to invest in the product.
