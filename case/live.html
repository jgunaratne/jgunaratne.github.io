Case Study: Gemini Live

  • Role Design Lead and Front-End Engineer
  • Stakeholders Product, Engineering and UX Directors, VPs, CEO
  • Timeframe 8 months

Problem

The increasing capabilities of AI models like Gemini presented an opportunity to create more natural and intuitive ways for users to interact with technology. However, prototyping multimodal user interfaces (UIs) that heavily rely on voice and nuanced conversational interactions posed significant challenges. The existing tools and APIs weren't fully equipped to simulate realistic conversations, accurately represent system states, or convey the sense of a "living" AI. This project, Gemini Live, aimed to address these challenges by developing a prototype for a conversational AI assistant that felt natural, responsive, and engaging. The urgency stemmed from the desire to showcase the potential of Gemini at a key event like Google I/O, demonstrating Google's leadership in conversational AI.

Results

  • Innovative use of visualization for AI — Through use of complex graphics shaders this UI visualization was a first of its kind at Google.
  • Led junior design engineers — I led a team of junior design engineers in the creation and iteration of the UI visualizations over tight deadlines.
  • Worked closely with design leadership — Design iterations required close collaboration with design directors and VP level leadership.
  • Positive reviews from major media publications — The Wall Street Journal, amongst others, featured Gemini Live on its front page with a largely positive review.
