Case Study: Material Design

  • Role Product Design and Prototyping Lead
  • Stakeholders VP of Design and Design Directors
  • Timeframe 1 year

Problem

Material Design represents Google's company-wide design system, which is also open-source. The end goal of this project involved creating a coherent design system with sample apps to highlight its capabilities for our VP of Design. Additionally, this work encompassed demonstrating inventive approaches for expanding the design system from its initial version. Our design director actively encouraged us to engage in prototyping and explore fresh elements within the design system.

Explorations included trying to apply real time graphic effects and filters to the user interface.

Research

I collaborated closely with quantitative user researchers who conducted tests on various components in the design system studying factors like readability, legibility, and accessibility to ensure the design system met user needs. Simultaneously, qualitative researchers examined how individuals perceived the visual design elements of the design system. Our team introduced proposals for innovative motion design and creative techniques to illustrate shadows, hierarchy, and z-index values within the design system. This comprehensive approach allowed us to refine and enhance the design system based on both quantitative and qualitative user feedback.

Personas

The design system served both internal Google users and external users including: internal developers utilizing the component code within our system; internal designers seeking comprehensive documentation and guidance to ensure adherence to the visual and motion elements of the design system; external developers using and contributing to the open source code on Github; and finally external designers seeking information and resources from the Google Material Design website.

Ideation

Ideation began with the original Material Design system and evolved into Material Design 2. Explorations to advance the design system included prototyping motion design and shadows to enhance the user experience. I created multiple prototypes to showcase dynamic animations on scrubbers and experimented with different visual effects and transitions, amongst other things. Additionally, I examined using creative applications of shapes in the user interface, exploring innovative ways to integrate and utilize them. These experiments and iterations constituted vital steps in refining and evolving the design, ensuring that Material Design 2 not only met functional requirements but also brought a visually engaging and dynamic quality to the user interface.

I explored different shadow effects to illustrate hierarchy and depth in the user interface.

I experimented with different shapes for customization and to allow for more creative design applications.

Mocks and Prototypes

To showcase our design system I developed various demo apps accessible through our Github code repository. These demo apps served a dual purpose: they not only allowed users to interact with the design system, but were also employed in design specifications to illustrate the application of Material Design beyond Google, without incorporating Google branding. Additionally, I designed an internal catalog application specifically tailored to demonstrate the Material Design components. This internal catalog played a crucial role in presenting the capabilities of Material Design to our team of designers and engineers within Google, providing a hands-on experience with the design system's features and functionalities.

The Material Design Catalog app was designed to showcase the design system's components and features.

User Testing

In the final round of user testing for the design system, quantitative user researchers gathered direct feedback from Amazon Mechanical Turk users, focusing on accessibility and readability features. This resulted in some colors used in the design system becoming stronger, and required improving contrast and increasing font size on some design components.

Qualitative research exposed the challenges of internal users who needed more design system documentation. The research also indicated that many Google teams needed to adopt Material Design in a phased way over time. The research found this particularly true for non-consumer facing enterprise applications used by Google Ads clients and internal tools. These more risk averse clients wanted to temper their adoption of a new design system due to the revenue-critical aspects of Google Ads at the company.

Final Design

VP of Design, Matias Duarte, reviewed the final design system specifications, prototypes and demos. Material Design 2 marked a significant milestone as the first of Google's design systems to gain prominent attention in the press, garnering positive responses for its innovative and enhanced features. This recognition underscored the success of the design team's efforts in refining and advancing the design system to a level that not only met internal standards but also captured external acclaim.

The Material Design Shrine app was released on GitHub to showcase the design system's capabilities. It served to showcase the design system's robust use outside of Google's branding.

Results

  • Authored widely used components — Material Design components I designed and wrote are used in most Google products both internally and externally. This includes both consumer and enterprise products.
  • Established strong cross product relationships — Being part of the core Material Design team I worked closely with many Google product teams to advise and influence component use.
  • Influenced design systems across industry — My work not only influenced work at Google, but its open source nature allowed it to influence design systems across the industry.
